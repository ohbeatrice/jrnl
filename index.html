Who is a Journalist?

A journalist is someone who investigates, collects and presents information as a news story. News stories can be presented through newspapers, magazines, radio, television and the internet.

What is News?

What makes a story “newsworthy” is generally based on the following criteria:

  • Is it new?
  • Is it interesting or significant?
  • Is it unusual?
  • Is it about people?

    • Types of News Stories

    Breaking News

    Telling about an important event as it happens.

    Feature Stories

    A detailed look at something interesting that's not breaking news.

    Investigative Stories

    Investigative journalism is when reporters deeply investigate a single topic such as serious crimes, political corruption, or corporate wrongdoing.

    When is it News or Opinion?

    Journalism is writing that tells people what happens. Sometimes, this writing comes out as a well-supported personal point-of-view or opinion.

    Opinion Journalism Examples

    Editorials

    Unsigned articles that express a publication's opinion.

    Columns

    Signed articles that express the writer's reporting and his conclusions.

    Reviews

    Such as concert, restaurant or movie reviews.

    Cartoons

    Many editorial cartoons offer opinionated political commentary in the form of satire or jokes.
